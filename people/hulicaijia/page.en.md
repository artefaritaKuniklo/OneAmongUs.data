## Prologue

When Caijia and I were still in Chongqing, we often went to the restaurant downstairs for douhua rice.
The owner recognized me every time.
We'd become quite familiar with each other.

In Sichuan and Chongqing, douhua rice is always eaten with something spicy.
The douhua itself has hardly any flavour.

Roasted Chili Sauce is pungent — every bite burns.
Use a different condiment, and it takes on a different taste entirely.

> Caijia, just a kid.
>
> ...To me, she was something like douhua.
>
> Douhua has no flavour of its own. Whatever it is seasoned with, that is the flavour it takes on.

At the start of this year, I had the chance to return to Yubei.
The owner was still there, greeting me with the same smile as always.
But the person who once sat beside me was gone.
There would be no one to share a bowl of douhua rice with me ever again.

## Meeting

Caijia, just a kid.
When I first got to know her in early 2024, it was only because she was dealing medications.
What was it about her that drew me in...?

Back then, my profile picture was Gwyndolin from *Dark Souls*.
When she asked if my avatar was from Dark Souls, I said yes, and that rush of joy — finding someone who shared the same interest — welled up inside me.
I don't quite know what compelled me, but I listened to so many of the stories she told, and felt a kind of pity for her.
Perhaps it was also my own loneliness.
We started calling each other sisters, growing closer and closer, and she changed her QQ profile picture to Yorshka — Gwyndolin's sister.

She loved listening to Vocaloid songs and was always getting me to listen to all sorts of Vocaloid songs with her.
She also loved *Connect*, the opening theme of *Puella Magi Madoka Magica*.
She really did love Madoka.
In her mind, everyone who died would be taken in by the Law of Cycles.[^1]

When Caijia was still in Hunan, I once ordered drink delivery for her, and that was how I came to have her home address memorized.
One time, I heard she had attempted suicide.
In a panic, I gave the address to the local police.
It was only when the police got back to me and said she had vomited up the pills she'd swallowed, right in front of them, that I felt some relief.

## Soulmate

After we'd known each other for a while, I asked Caijia to come from Hunan to Chongqing to live with me.
She really was just a kid, I suppose I “tricked” her into coming to my place.

Caijia said she'd come, no problem.
But I wasn't allowed to stop her from cutting herself, wasn't allowed to stop her from overdosing, and wasn't allowed to stop her from — doing compensated dating.
All I could do was nod and go along with it, though in the end she never actually went through with that last one.

On 13th March, Caijia took the high-speed rail to come find me, but she'd bought a ticket to the remote Chongqingxi Station by mistake.
Still, I took the metro there early and waited.
When she finally arrived, the little lost soul got turned around again, and we spent ages searching for each other before we finally met.

The moment I saw her, something inside me just felt: “Ah, this must be may soulmate.”
Then I took her hand, and we rode the metro home.

## Mint

With her connections as a medication dealer, we often bought pills and took them together.
But I didn't realize that this was only ever a fleeting kind of happiness.

I remember that before she came to see me, she'd always been curious about what clonazepam tasted like.
I told her it tasted like mint, and you could even dissolve it under your tongue like candy.

The first night she stayed at my place, she couldn't quite settle in — maybe it was the unfamiliar bed.
She kept tossing and turning, so I tipped a clonazepam tablet into my mouth, leaned in, and kissed it into hers.

She seemed a little surprised.
She hadn't expected me to do that.
When the taste registered, she realized it really was just like mint.

## Conflict

Her emotions were very unstable.
Whenever she broke down, she'd rush into the bathroom, lock the door, and cut her arms and thighs with a surgical blade.
Sometimes it only took a disagreement between us for her to lock herself away and self-harm.
I was completely helpless, and it drove me to the edge too, with nowhere to let it out
All I could do was hurt myself the way she did.
The scars on my left hand are still clearly visible to this day.

Once, during a fight, I tried to wrestle the blade away from her because she was cutting herself.
She wanted to jump off the building; I held her back.
But I wasn't as strong as her, and seeing that it wasn't working, I had no choice but to call the police, hoping that would calm her down.
That night, Caijia was taken to the Chongqing Mental Health Centre, where she stayed overnight.

The next day I went to see her, only to be told that non-family members weren't allowed in for visits.
So I waited outside, and waited, for her family to come.
By evening, her grandfather and father arrived.
I met with them; they went in and spoke with the doctors, and Caijia was released.
Her family told me they hoped I could take good care of her, and reminded Caijia not to cause trouble.
They stayed in Chongqing for one day and then went back. They actually seemed like decent people.

When Caijia and I were together, we sometimes talked about the future.
I told her we should study Japanese together, that one day we'd go to Japan.
I even discussed the plan with her mother and put together a proper proposal.
Her mother treated her well enough.
After I mediated for a while, her mother came to accept her gender identity and even bought her girls' clothes.
She was a gentle person.

## Companionship

Looking back carefully now, the time I spent with her in Chongqing was probably less than two months.
But she truly did have a happy time.

She loved eating Cuixiangmi instant noodle snacks, tomato-flavoured instant noodles, and Fujiya lollipops.
Every trip to the convenience store, she'd grab a whole armful and carry it all home.
She also loved going to McDonald's with me, and tucked into Grilled Chicken Sandwich and McFlurries.

Caijia loved watching anime.
Back then, we finished watching *Gushing over Magical Girls* together.
The title sounds like a magical girl story, but it's actually overflowing with strange yuri elements.
Her favourite character was Araga Kiwi, someone who looks like a fool on the surface but is actually hiding a great deal of strength.
Sometimes, after taking our pills, we'd lie on the sofa, hook the laptop up to the TV, and keep enjoying the scene.

We sometimes bought Rio premixed cocktails and watched *Kamen Rider Build* together.
She quite liked it. The scene that left the deepest impression on her was Saruwatari Kazumi's final transformation. His Hazard Level[^2] was too high, and in order to save his companions and the world, he forced a transformation using the Blizzard Knuckle, ultimately dying because it exceeded what a human body could withstand.

In our free time, we'd also play games together.
She loved the Dark Souls series and its fan-made game, BLACK SOULS.
We played *Dark Souls 1* together — after all, it was our profile pictures that brought us together in the first place.
I'd actually gotten into Dark Souls ages ago; I was a veteran.
Sometimes, when she couldn't beat a boss, I'd just sit right down at the computer desk:

“Let me handle this! There's no boss your big sis can't beat!”

We went to so many places, and those are all memories worth cherishing. Guanyinqiao,[^3] the Jiefangbei Pedestrian Street[^4]... we had fun in so many places.
It was a kind of happiness I hadn't felt in a long time — as if I wasn't so alone anymore.

I remember walking with her along the streets around Jiefangbei, around eight or nine in the evening, just holding her hand, and yet it felt like I had the whole world.

I remember that even though we were still underage, we snuck into a bar and ordered a drink.
I think it was a Blue Margarita[^5] — my first drink at a bar, and hers too.

I remember one time we went out to eat, over by the shopping district near Chongqing Central Park.[^6]
We'd probably been cooped up at home too long and just wanted something nice.

> “Where shall we go this time? Jiefangbei? Or Central Park?”
>
> “Let's go to Central Park — it's closer.”
>
> “Whatever you say~”

Downstairs from Paradise Walk, there was a big figurine shop.
Caijia and I browsed around in there for ages — so many cute figurines, all of which we wanted and none of which we could afford.

It was quite a feast, and Caijia was very happy.
Though perhaps girls really do have four stomachs — one for the main course, one for snacks, one for fruit, and one for drinks.
After the meal, we left the Paradise Walk and went looking for something to drink.

At Naìsnow, Caijia and I placed our order and sat down to wait until the staff called our number.
Right at that moment, her mother happened to call to ask how Caijia was doing.

## Plushies

29th April was my birthday, and Caijia had prepared a plushie for me well in advance. It was a... big shark.

But Caijia, clumsy as she was, plunged the knife in a bit too hard while opening the package and accidentally stabbed the poor sharkie to death.

Caijia was a little upset.

“It's okay, it'll be fine,” I comforted her.

While she turned and went back to her room, I dug out the sewing kit my mother had left behind, followed a tutorial online, and stitched the sharkie back together.

“Look, look — it's alive again!”

But Caijia still felt guilty, thinking it was her fault for ruining my birthday present.
Later, to make up for it, she bought me a Kuromi plushie.

## Swarm

She kept listening to some “doctor” in the overdose community, and after hearing that amantadine was good, she'd often buy it behind my back and take it, which left her in a terrible mental state.
She said she kept seeing hallucinations — seeing insects — and I had no idea what to do.
All I could do was throw away her pills and stay by her side.

> I'm really scared of bugs...
>
> The hallucinations from "goodnight"[^7] are really strange and terrifying.
>
> Swarms of insects pouring out from under the bed, the floor covered in shattered glass from my glasses...
>
> Goodnight — what irony...
>
> (Excerpted from Caijia's tweets)

Then there was another time... I think it was because I didn't want her using the surgical blade to hurt herself anymore, and we ended up in a physical struggle.
She beat me black and blue. I was falling apart and just wanted to go to my room for some quiet.
She thought I was going to do something stupid and broke the lock on my bedroom door, then came crashing in.

Both our families found out about this, and they demanded that Caijia go back to Hunan.
But I couldn't bear to let her go, seeing the state she was in, and Caijia felt bad about what she'd done and couldn't bear to leave me either.
So together we made up a lie. We told them she had gone somewhere else to visit a friend, and she actually stayed on at my place.

## Insomnia

On 4th May, it was drizzling in Yubei.
I'd been up all night gaming.

Caijia came up to me and asked: “Sis hasn't been overdosing lately...What's going on...?”

Looking back, maybe I'd unconsciously felt that she was in too bad a state and thought it would be better not to touch the stuff anymore.
I can't quite remember now. But she brought it up, and I thought, well, if that's what she wants.

“I guess you're right. If you want some, I'll go buy with you.” I said.

On the way home from buying the pills, she picked up a parcel — a box of empty capsule shells.
According to her, if you crushed the pills and put them inside, they wouldn't taste so bad. We took them together after that.

...What on earth was I doing...

Then I felt like I couldn't sleep, so I took some clonazepam and tried to lie down in bed.
But right around that time, her phone and ID card went missing.

“We'll look for them later. I'm so tired, so sleepy... let me just rest for a bit.”

Maybe it was because the clonazepam hit too hard. By the time I woke up again, I was already in an ambulance.
Caijia leaned in close and told me she'd been calling my name but couldn't wake me, so she used my phone to call 120.

“What happened... I'm awake, aren't I? Just a bit drowsy...”
Since there was nothing seriously wrong, I paid the ambulance fee, and Caijia and I went home to keep resting.

When I woke up again it was three or four in the morning, and I realized my phone was gone.
Using iCloud's Find My feature, I saw it was located at the police station.
I threw on some clothes and hurried over, and sure enough, there she was.

She was wearing clothes I'd never seen before.
I later found out it was because she'd been running around outside wearing only a top, and someone had brought her to the station.

“Sis, what are you doing at the police station? Aren't you coming home? Let's go home and sleep!”
My own head was still a bit foggy.

So I took my phone back, held Caijia's hand, and we went home.

## Shattered

“I still want to play games. Can you still play Dark Souls?”
Caijia asked me after we got home.

So much had been happening, and I still felt so drowsy, so desperate to rest.
All I could do was try to soothe her for a moment, then turn off the lights and go to sleep.

I went downstairs in a daze.
A few people who looked like security guards — or maybe police — led me forward.

“What's going on... it's so early. Where's my sis?”

They led me to the sports area in front of the property management office.
In the corner, there was a motionless figure.

My mind went blank. What is this...? Who is this...?

The figure had its back to me.
I looked carefully at the top she was wearing — the only piece of clothing on her — and suddenly jolted awake from the haze.

“Sis! What happened to you! Sis! Answer me! Please, just respond...”
I kept calling out to her, hoping for any kind of reaction.

But there was no response.

The security guards around her wouldn't let me get close.

“Oh dear, she's already gone. The ambulance already came, took one look, and left. There's no point trying to save her now.” The woman nearby sighed, then turned away, unwilling to deal with me any further.

The police urged me to leave too, saying I needed to go do something or other, telling me to stop looking.

But I couldn't bring myself to go.
My legs felt like they were filled with lead — so heavy — and I stood rooted to the spot.

They walked over and turned Caijia onto her back. Only then could I finally see her face.

The tears wouldn't stop.

“I'm sorry... your big sis didn't protect you...”

## Absence

After I finished giving my statement, the officers at the station were worried I'd have a breakdown — that I'd be alone in Chongqing, unable to cope, and try to end my own life.
So I was kept there for two days, until my mother came all the way from Xinjiang to Chongqing to take me out.

“Have you eaten?”

“I don't want to eat anything. I have absolutely no appetite...”

That's what I said, but later, I still went to the McDonald's downstairs and bought a McFlurry.

“Caijia... she loved these.”

“Don't bring that up.”

Walking back towards home, I deliberately made my way to that little corner of the sports area.
It seemed to have been cleaned up.
You couldn't tell at all what had happened there just the morning before.

A life. Cordoned off with police tape, covered with a tarp, the crowd dispersed. Before long, it was as though the whole thing had never happened.

Those who witnessed the tragedy — would any of them take it to heart?
They all scattered soon enough.

The crowds came and went as always, laughter and footsteps interweaving without pause.
In the end, I alone remembered what had happened in this corner where parents and children play.

## Yubei

When Caijia left, she still had two packs of unfinished tomato instant noodles.

Because of what had happened, my family took me away and kept me under watch.
Later, due to family circumstances, I left Chongqing for good.

That home in Yubei is a home I can never return to.
It holds my youth, and it holds the most painful thing that has ever happened to me.

In March, I went back to the old place in Yubei.
I brought her a few bottles of compound liquorice tablets.

I miss the days of mixing premixed cocktails with cola, taking liquorice tablets and DXM.
And that little path we'd walk to pick up parcels.

Does she remember any of this?
Wherever she is — has she found happiness...?

In the blink of an eye, the name Yubei has been fading with the wind.
The signs bearing Yubei's name have been taken down one by one, replaced by Liangjiang New Area.[^8]

But that story remains forever in a Yubei that can never be returned to.

## Last Chapter

The overdose community had too great an influence on Caijia.
If not for that, she might have had a decent future.

> Caijia, just a kid.
>
> ...To me, she was something like douhua.
>
> Whoever she was with — that's who she'd become...

After Caijia was gone, I never ordered anything else at bars.
Only Blue Margaritas. Every time I go to a bar, that's all I order.
Even though I don't actually like drinking them at all... the rim of the glass is lined with sea salt — bitter and astringent.

But it was my first drink at a bar, and it was hers too.
